Knowledge Base - Raising Chicks

Everything you need to know about raising healthy, productive chicks

üê£

Before Your Chicks Arrive

  • Prepare brooder area 24-48 hours before arrival
  • Set up heat lamp to maintain 95¬∞F (35¬∞C)
  • Have starter feed and fresh water ready
  • Line brooder with clean pine shavings
  • Check for drafts and predators
üå°Ô∏è

Temperature Management

  • Week 1: 95¬∞F (35¬∞C)
  • Week 2: 90¬∞F (32¬∞C)
  • Week 3: 85¬∞F (29¬∞C)
  • Week 4: 80¬∞F (27¬∞C)
  • Week 5: 75¬∞F (24¬∞C)
  • Week 6: 70¬∞F (21¬∞C)
  • Reduce by 5¬∞F each week until room temperature
üì¶

Essential Supplies

  • Brooder box or enclosure
  • Heat lamp with red bulb
  • Thermometer
  • Starter feed (20-22% protein)
  • Waterers and feeders
  • Bedding material
  • Chick starter grit
üè†

Brooder Setup

  • Size: 2 square feet per chick
  • Height: 18-24 inches tall
  • Material: Cardboard, plastic, or wood
  • Ventilation: Small openings for air flow
  • Location: Draft-free, predator-safe area
  • Bedding: 2-3 inches of pine shavings
üí°

Lighting Requirements

  • 24 hours light for first week
  • Gradually reduce to 16 hours by week 4
  • Use red bulbs to reduce pecking
  • Position heat lamp safely above brooder
  • Provide dark areas for rest
  • Avoid bright white lights
üßπ

Cleaning & Maintenance

  • Remove wet bedding daily
  • Complete bedding change weekly
  • Clean and sanitize waterers daily
  • Wash feeders every 2-3 days
  • Monitor for ammonia smell
  • Keep brooder dry and draft-free
üåæ

Starter Feed Guidelines

  • Protein: 20-22% for first 6-8 weeks
  • Feed ad lib (always available)
  • Use shallow feeders for easy access
  • Avoid medicated feed unless necessary
  • Store feed in cool, dry place
  • Check for mold or pests
üíß

Water Management

  • Fresh water available 24/7
  • Water temperature: 50-60¬∞F (10-15¬∞C)
  • Use chick-sized waterers
  • Add marbles to prevent drowning
  • Clean and refill daily
  • Consider electrolytes for first 3 days
ü•Ñ

Feeding Schedule

  • Day 1-3: Starter feed only
  • Week 1: Introduce chick grit
  • Week 2: Small greens occasionally
  • Week 4: Reduce protein to 18-20%
  • Week 6: Transition to grower feed
  • Monitor feed consumption daily
üíâ

Vaccination Schedule

  • Day 1: Marek's disease (if not done at hatchery)
  • Week 1: Newcastle disease
  • Week 2: Infectious bronchitis
  • Week 4: Gumboro disease
  • Week 6: Fowl pox (if endemic area)
  • Consult local vet for regional requirements
üîç

Daily Health Checks

  • Observe activity levels
  • Check for bright, clear eyes
  • Monitor breathing (no wheezing)
  • Check for clean vent area
  • Watch for abnormal droppings
  • Ensure all chicks are eating and drinking
üö®

Warning Signs

  • Lethargy or isolation
  • Loss of appetite
  • Diarrhea or bloody droppings
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Swollen eyes or nasal discharge
  • Abnormal posture or movement
üå°Ô∏è

Temperature Issues

  • Too cold: Chicks huddle, chirp loudly
  • Too hot: Chicks spread out, pant
  • Just right: Active, evenly distributed
  • Adjust heat lamp height accordingly
  • Use multiple thermometers to verify
  • Check for drafts causing cold spots
üçΩÔ∏è

Feeding Problems

  • Not eating: Check temperature, water access
  • Pasting: Clean vent area gently
  • Overeating: Monitor feed levels
  • Food aggression: Add more feeders
  • Wasting feed: Adjust feeder height
  • Ensure feed is fresh and dry
üò∑

Common Issues

  • Coccidiosis: Bloody droppings, lethargy
  • Respiratory: Sneezing, discharge
  • Impacted crop: Hard crop area
  • Splay leg: Legs spread apart
  • Pasting up: Droppings block vent
  • Consult vet for persistent issues
